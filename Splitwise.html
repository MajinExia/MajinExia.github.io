Background

The Splitwise Feature Design is a project for an Interaction Design university course that aims to fimilarize ourselves with working on an existing design system before developing a new design system in a later project with Fureverhome. My team and I were assigned to research and create a new feature for Splitwise, a payment app that automatically calculates multiple bills and splits expenses for a group of people.

Process

Research

To start our research for the app, we first looked at the app itself, studying its existing design system and interaction. Afterwards, each of us conducted secondary research online to find potential problem with Splitwise currently, this would help us narrow down our scope and assist us with indentifying what we wanted to look at during our primary research being user interviews and observing users interacting with the app.

Competitive Analysis

During our research, we also looked at other expense spliting application to help us understand what features Splitwise have and how they compare with others in the same market

User Interviews & Initial Ideas

To get a better understanding what frustrations current users have with Splitwise, we conducted two rounds of user interviews with 7 participants each times. While there were various issue and potential improvements to the app that the interviewee would like to be implemented, we found common responses highlighted in the photo below

Through interviewing users, our team came up with 3 initial ideas: A preferred payment info to let people know how to pay someone, an import spreadsheet feature to help new users coming from other expense tracking app and finally a location based currecy feature that automatically changes the currency based on the user's location and numbering format of a specific currency.

Final Proposal

Selected Idea and Persona

After doing a second round of user interviews in combination with feedback from the teaching staff, our team decided to go ahead with the payment information idea with an added functionality being a 2-way verification feature. And in the process, to help us visualize our users, we formed final persona below.

Feature overview

The "Payment information and 2-way verification" feature lets users see their friends' preferred payment methods when settling debts. Additionally, through real-time updates, users can see the status of their payments and know precisely when they have been received. Simplifying the process of managing shared expenses streamlines the process and ensures peace of mind for all users involved

Reflection

Through working on this project, I have gain experience with working with an existing visual and interaction design system. It has also reaffirm the importance of designing for actual users needs and not simply using assumption from the design team!
