Background

POV: You're a Karamatsu Girl is collborative project between me and 4 other group members with the goal of creating a self-insert music video for one of Yorushika's song, Ano Natsu ni Sake (English: Blooming in That Summer). The story revolves around our protagonist (first person POV: it's you!) one night manages to interact with their beloved anime character in a dream and they fall in love across the dimensions! Both of them try their absolute best to somehow get back and see each other again from their respective worlds.

Process

Visualizing cinematography: Shotlist & storyboarding

The very first step that we needed to take was to break down the song into verses and create a storyboard to visualize what scenes/models we needed to create and animate. In this stage, I was in charge of writing the shot list and storyboarding the third chorus up until the ending of the song with below being some storyboard that I was in charge of.

Modelling

In between iterating our storyboard, each one of us started on modelling the different objects we would need to include in our animation. I was in charge of modelling the main items in our protagonist's room that they will interact with, namely the night stand and the desk setup which includes the desk itself, computer screens, a mouse and keyboard and finally the keyboard itself.

Challenge

Maya nParticles issue

In the outro scene of our animation, I wanted to simulate a gush of wind blowing sakura petals from outside to make the curtains move and flower petals go from the outside into the room through the use of Maya's nParticles and nCloth systems respectively. However, in the process, I ran into issues with the nParticles systems, namely it not displaying the flower petals properly but instead showed a white sphere even though we added the flower object so that Maya would be able to generate more flower petals flying into the shot. Another issue was that the nCloth effect not simulating properly during render although it was working as intended in the viewport render, as it would stay at its first frame position as the scene goes on.

With the deadline of the project coming closer and closer, I decided to do a work around and animated one Sakura petal in Maya and simulate the effect in After Effects using the CC Particle world effect to essentially achieve the same results.

Final Results!

After working on the animation for over a month in total, our team was able to showcase our fanmade 3D music video in our class's final showcase. We received great praises from our instructors and peers, and were even awarded the "Rendering Revive" award for our creative camera movement and lighting in creating a POV 3D animation! You can watch our final animation here! And for more details about our project including the initial animatic and character design, visit our project website!

Reflection

As this was my first time 3D modelling and animating, I definitely feel that I could have done some parts more efficiently such as just using a transparent object to simulate the water dropplet instead of using Bifrost as it would make rendering the scene easier. However, through this project, I have learned the basic principles of 3D modelling and animation which could be transfered over a different 3D modelling program such as Blender.
