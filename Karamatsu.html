POV: You're a Karamatsu Girl

A fanmade 3D Music Video made in Autodesk Maya made for a 3rd year university course

Roles

Modeller, Animator

Type

Group Project

Tools

After Effects, Autodesk Maya

Background

POV: You're a Karamatsu Girl is collborative project between me and 4 other group members with a goal to create a self-insert music video for one of Yorushika's song, Ano Natsu ni Sake (English: Blooming in That Summer). Our protagonist (first person POV: it's you!) one night manages to interact with their beloved anime character in a dream and they fall in love across the dimensions! Both of them try their absolute best to somehow get back and see each other again from their respective worlds.

Process

Visualizing cinematography: Shotlist & storyboarding

As one of my teammates have had the concept for this project in mind for a few years now, the very first step that we needed to take was to break down the song into verses to visualize what scenes/models we needed to create and animate. In this stage, I was in charge of writing the shot list and storyboarding the third chorus up until the ending of the song

Modelling

In between iterating our storyboard, each one of us started on modelling the different objects we would need to include in our animation. I was in charge of modelling the main items in our protagonist's room that they will interact with, namely the night stand and the desk setup which includes the desk itself, computer screens, a mouse and keyboard and finally the keyboard itself.

Challenge

During the process of creating the animation, I encountered various issues regarding the different effect system and rendering process. Below are a few notable issues I came across and the solutions I have came up to address it!

Maya Bifrost issue

To animate Ren and Ayumi in this motion graphic, I decided to use the puppet pin tool built into After Effect as the character animation that I had in mind could be done most efficiently using the puppet pin tool. In addition, as the club could potentially reuse the animation in the future, using a feature built into After Effects would help the graphic designer from SFU Anime get up to speed quickly as they would be able to edit and make changes to the animation if request the original After Effects files.

After animating the first mascot using the puppet pin tool, I applied the same principle to the other mascot and animated the mouths of the two characters to add more detail to the animation using a combination of the puppet pin tool and the pen tool to create a fill background so it doesn't look out of place as the area behind the moving parts are blank in the original illustration.

To make it even more authentic, I have added the sakura leaves falling from the sakura tree using the particle world effect and the raccoon (the unofficial mascot of SFU) going towards the piece of mochi to eat to add a bit more spice and simulate real life as if the characters were actually in a Japanese park during the cherry blossom season.

Reflection

In creating this short motion graphic animation, I have gained an understanding of basic character animation in after effects using the puppet pin tool. After doing this project, I look forward to doing more character animation in after effects as it's given me the ability to breathe in life in a still 2D character. In particular, I was also able to apply this ability to animate the mascot of the SFU Anime's annual summer festival in a teaser trailer I created for them!

However, looking back, I would have loved to try out more methods of animation within After Effects as the puppet pin tool that I used have limitations on how it can be used as the puppet pin tool distort the layer in order to simulate animation and might not be applicable with every situation.
