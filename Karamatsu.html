Background

POV: You're a Karamatsu Girl is collborative project between me and 4 other group members with the goal of creating a self-insert music video for one of Yorushika's song, Ano Natsu ni Sake (English: Blooming in That Summer). Our protagonist (first person POV: it's you!) one night manages to interact with their beloved anime character in a dream and they fall in love across the dimensions! Both of them try their absolute best to somehow get back and see each other again from their respective worlds.

Process

Visualizing cinematography: Shotlist & storyboarding

The very first step that we needed to take was to break down the song into verses and create a storyboard to visualize what scenes/models we needed to create and animate. In this stage, I was in charge of writing the shot list and storyboarding the third chorus up until the ending of the song.

Modelling

In between iterating our storyboard, each one of us started on modelling the different objects we would need to include in our animation. I was in charge of modelling the main items in our protagonist's room that they will interact with, namely the night stand and the desk setup which includes the desk itself, computer screens, a mouse and keyboard and finally the keyboard itself.

Challenge

During the process of creating the animation, I encountered various issues regarding the different effect system and rendering process. Below are a few notable issues I came across and the solutions I have came up to address it!

Maya Bifrost issue

In our animation, I wanted to use Maya's built in effects system "Bifrost" to create realistic water particles and to simulate physics in a 3D environment. However, this decision would cause me issues later on when rendering and animating the scenes. In no particular order, here are 2 notable issues I've encountered when working with Bifrost. Firstly, when animating the scene, the Bifrost liquid would disappear from the viewports even though the bounding box for the object is still there.

The second and argubly most frustrating issue was the rendering issue I encountered with the Bifrost scene which was that I couldn't render my scene as a sequence in Maya as whenever I initilize the render, it would throw me an error. After researching online for a solution to this issue, I found that this error code has been an issue for sevevral years with no real fix aside from redoing everything from scratch and hope it works the next time around.

Despite this issue, I found that I was able to still render the scene only if I do it manually on a frame by frame basis using the Arnold render view. However, through doing this, I encountered a different issue where Maya would just randomly crash which caused a significant setback in the rendering process. Unfortunately, I couldn't solve the issue aside from brute forcing it to render through trial and error.

Maya nParticles issue

In the outro scene of our animation, I wanted to simulate a gush of wind blowing sakura petals from outside to make the curtains move and flower petals go from the outside into the room through the use of Maya's nParticles and nCloth systems respectively. However, in the process, I ran into issues with the nParticles systems, namely it not displaying the flower petals properly but instead showed a white sphere even though we added the flower object so that Maya would be able to generate more flower petals flying into the shot. Another issue was that the nCloth effect not simulating properly during render although it was working as intended in the viewport, as it would stay at its first frame position as the scene goes on.

After wasting too many days watching youtube tutorials and troubleshooting the nParticles issue, I wasn't able to resolve the issues. However, as a quick fix to the nParticles system, I decided to just animate one Sakura petal and do the rest in After Effects using the CC Particle world effect. In hindsight, I should have done this from the beginning as it took way too long for what its worth.

Final Results!

After working on the animation for over a month in total, our team was able to showcase our fanmade 3D music video in our class's final showcase. We received great praises from our instructors and peers, and were even awarded the "Rendering Revive" award for our creative camera movement and lighting in creating a POV 3D animation! You can watch our final animation here! And for more details about our project including the animatic and character design, visit our project website!

Reflection

As this was my first time 3D modelling and animating, I definitely feel that I could have done some parts more efficiently such as just using a transparent object to simulate the water dropplet instead of using Bifrost as it would make rendering the scene easier. However, through this project, I have learned the basic principles of 3D modelling and animation which could be transfered over a different 3D modelling program such as Blender. This project also sparked my interests in further learing 3D modelling and creating different renders (Blender Donut tutorial here I come!)
